# UI-UX-Experiment5

## Aim:
To understand and apply the process of identifying user experience (UX) problems, selecting appropriate research methods, and developing user personas for different project scenarios such as a Health Tracker App and Government Service Portal
## Algorithm:

## STEP 1:
Understand the Scenario Analyze the given UX design scenarios — Health Tracker App and Government Service Portal — to identify the target users, their needs, and overall goals for improving user experience.

## STEP 2: 
Identify User Pain Points Observe or assume realistic challenges faced by users in each scenario.

For the wellness app, focus on stress management, emotional tracking, and motivation.

For the waste management app, focus on communication gaps, reporting issues, and service inefficiency.

## STEP 3:
Write UX Problem Statements Formulate problem statements using the UX template: "[User] needs a way to [achieve goal] because [obstacle], which results in [impact]." Develop two clear problem statements for each scenario that highlight specific user difficulties.

## STEP 4:
Select Research Methods Identify suitable research methods to explore user needs and validate design solutions. For example:

Mental Wellness App: User interviews, diary studies, usability testing.

Waste Management App: Surveys, field observation, contextual inquiry.

## STEP 5:
Justify Method Selection Explain the rationale for each method, showing how it helps gather meaningful insights about user behavior, emotional challenges, and environmental interactions.

## STEP 6:
Create User Personas Based on realistic assumptions or existing data, create at least two user personas per scenario. Include: Demographics (age, occupation, location) Goals and motivations Behaviors and habits Pain points and frustrations Quotes or statements representing user perspective

## STEP 7: 
Summarize Findings Summarize how the identified pain points, problem statements, research methods, and personas connect to guide user-centered design decisions for both projects. Highlight how the proposed solutions aim to improve usability, accessibility, and user satisfaction.

## Scenario 1: Health Tracker Mobile App 

## Problem Statements
User Pain Points (from UI)

Users may forget to log activities (Log Activity screen)

Health stats may feel overwhelming (Health Stats screen)

Managing doctor appointments can be confusing

## Problem Statements

A health-conscious user needs a way to quickly log daily activities because manual tracking is time-consuming, which results in incomplete health data.

A patient needs a way to view health statistics clearly because complex charts are hard to understand, which results in poor health decisions.

## Research Methods
Methods Used

Usability Testing

Interviews

Diary Study

Justification

Usability Testing → Test screens like Log Activity and Appointments to ensure easy navigation

Interviews → Understand how users manage health and doctor visits

Diary Study → Track daily health logging behavior over time

## User Personas

Occupation: IT Professional

Goals

Maintain daily fitness goals

Behaviors

Motivation

## Persona 2: Meena Sharma (Doctor in UI)

Age: 40

Occupation: Cardiologist

Goals

Manage patient appointments

Monitor patient health data

Behaviors

Reviews patient stats

Uses appointment scheduling

Pain Points

Needs clear patient data visualization

Scheduling conflicts

Motivation

Provide better healthcare service

## Scenario 2: E-Commerce Grocery Delivery App

## Problem Statements


Difficulty finding products quickly

Complicated checkout process

Lack of trust in delivery time

## Problem Statements

A working parent needs a way to quickly order groceries because they have limited time, which results in frustration during long searches.

An online shopper needs a way to trust delivery schedules because delays are common, which results in reduced satisfaction.

## Research Methods

Selected Methods

Contextual Inquiry

Surveys

Usability Testing

Justification

Contextual Inquiry observes users in real shopping situations

Surveys identify common shopping habits and preferences

Usability Testing helps improve navigation and checkout flow

## User Personas
